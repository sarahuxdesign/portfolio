SunPass Redesign

UI/UX Design

Sunpass is a prepaid toll app based in Florida. I decided to do a solo exploration project to redesign the app's interface and dentify any usability issues.

OVERVIEW

SunPass is the Florida Department of Transportation’s innovative Prepaid Toll Program. SunPass customers typically pay less than cash customers at most toll plazas and exits on Turnpike roads. They currently have a website and app to for customers to pay their tolls.

The objective is to explore the human-centered design process and research the user needs in order to redesign the app that would result in increase consumer engagement and expansion of brand awareness.

My Role:
User Research, Interaction, Visual design, Prototyping & Testing

Duration:
Oct 2017 - Dec 2017

Tools:
Photoshop, Sketch, InDesign, InVision

THE CHALLENGE
What is the most strategic opportunity for Sunpass that will enhance a customer’s experience?

The Sunpass App’s functionality is limited compared to the website and customers have complained of low loading times and bug issues, resulting in a customer rating of 2.9/5 stars. Being Sunpass user myself, I downloaded the app to review its interface and functionality. I decided to take on this exploration project as practice redesigning and expanding an existing interface.

DESIGN PROCESS
USER RESEARCH
Identify the user and problems

According to Sunpass, only 81% of users participate in the Sunpass program. Participation peaked in urban areas where there are more commuters.

This application crashes constantly and leaves me having to pay from home. Not helpful when you’re on the road. Needs to be made more user-friendly and fixed all the bugs and glitches.

Guerrilla Testing

I gathered user feedback by taking my phone with the downloaded SunPass app into the Valencia Graphics Lab room and asked fellow students and Sunpass users for their thoughts.

Market Research

I viewed the Sunpass app on online app stores to review the feedbacks from reviews. The current score on the is 2.9/5 stars, and most complaints are due to functionality and usability problems.

Comparative Analyiss

To define problems of existing solutions, I conducted competitive analysis of existing applications that has similar functions and goals of Sunpass.

Peachpass

The Peach Pass is connected with the State Road and Tollway Authority (SRTA) and automatically deducts tolls when using the electronic toll lanes of the Georgia Express Lanes.

Strengths

  • Apparent signifiers and call to action

Weakness

Transurban Linkt

Established in 1996, Transurban is an Australian company that manages and develops toll road networks in Australia and the United States.

Strengths

Weakness

USER TESTING
Scenario 1: You got a new license plate. You need to edit the licence from your current vehicle.
Original Design

3/5 users can edit the license plate. The main complaint was having to click on the vehicle and then tapping the edit button to make changes.

New Design

5/5 can find the edit feature on the vehicles page.

Scenario 2: Your transponder malfunctioned and now you have to pay a fine. Find the pay toll feature.
Original Design

2/5 can find the pay toll feature. The main complaint is that it’s only available on the sign in screen.

New Design

5/5 could easily find the pay toll feature as it was on the bottom global navigation.

REFLECTION
Lessons learned

I very much enjoyed the design process, from ideation to validation, for the Sunpass app. During one user testing, someone pointed out the lack of a home button on the bottom bar, which is a crucial element on the app.

In this project, we are a relatively small team without much industry experience. It’s important for our team to narrow down the target audience and focus on the problem we are capable to solve. Our stakeholder came with a ambiguous project scope and we had to focus on being agile, work in small increments and aim to move fast.

Although it is easy to assume what is best for the product, I learned the importance of not relying on my own assumptions and to constantly validate my own ideas with user feedback.
